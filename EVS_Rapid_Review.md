# EVS Rapid Review: Key Exam Topics

This guide focuses specifically on high-priority topics and assignment questions for quick, pointwise exam revision.

### 1. Global Warming *(Assignment 1)*
*   **Primary Cause**: The continuous burning of fossil fuels and massive rates of deforestation, leading to the rapid buildup of greenhouse gases (GHGs).
*   **Greenhouse Effect Mechanism**: Sunlight enters Earth, but **GHGs like CO2, CH4, and N2O trap the returning heat**, acting like a thermal blanket.
*   **Feedback Mechanism**: As ice melts, less sunlight reflects into space (Albedo effect), causing oceans to absorb even more heat, rapidly accelerating global warming.
*   **Assignment Question (Polar Bears)**: Melting polar ice caps directly destroy the **sea ice hunting platforms** of polar bears. This leads to starvation, reduced reproduction, and drastically **decreases the polar bear population**.

### 2. Consumerism *(Assignment 1)*
*   **Definition**: The ideology and extreme addiction to buying and owning **luxury resources** and goods far beyond basic human needs.
*   **Environmental Impact**: Drives the **mass extraction** of raw materials, over-industrialization, and gigantic generation of non-biodegradable waste. 
*   **Consumerism vs Population**: A small population with **high per capita consumption** (rich developed nations) damages the environment faster than a large population with low utilization (poor developing nations).

### 3. Sustainability *(Assignments 1, 2, 3)*
*   **Definition**: Meeting current needs without compromising the ability of **future generations** to meet their own human needs.
*   **Pillars of Sustainable Development**: Built on three equal pillars—**Social Equity, Economic Viability, and Environmental Protection**.
*   **Challenges**: Overpopulation, extreme consumerism, severe poverty, and historical political inaction.
*   **Overcoming Challenges via Technology**: Exploring **green technology** like massive solar panel arrays, **bioplastics** (for packaging), and **biofertilizers** (to replace toxic chemical farming).

### 4. Food Web 
*   **Definition**: A highly complex network of **interconnected food chains** showing exactly "who eats whom" in an entire ecosystem.
*   **Resilience**: Multiple paths of energy flow make the ecosystem **highly stable**. If one species is suddenly removed, predators can switch to an alternate prey.
*   **Flow of Energy**: Energy flows primarily one-way from **Producers (plants)** to Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary **Consumers**, decreasing significantly at each level due to the strict 10% energy transfer rule.

### 5. Biogeochemical Cycles *(Assignment 2)*
*   **Carbon Cycle**: Plants absorb CO2 to make food (Photosynthesis); animals eat it and exhale CO2 (Respiration). **Human Interference**: Burning fossil fuels pumps too much CO2, overheating the Earth.
*   **Nitrogen Cycle**: Atmospheric Nitrogen (78%) is unusable directly. **Fixation**: Bacteria (like Rhizobium) or lightning convert it into usable soil nitrates for plants. **Denitrification** returns it back to the air.
*   **Phosphorus Cycle**: The only major cycle that **lacks an atmospheric phase**. Phosphorus travels incredibly slowly from **rock weathering** into soil, then plants, then animals, and finally washes deep into ocean beds.
*   **Water Cycle**: Driven entirely by solar energy. Involves continuous **Evaporation, Condensation, and Precipitation**, maintaining Earth's critical freshwater balance.

### 6. Biodiversity Conservation & Hotspots *(Assignment 2)*
*   **Biodiversity**: The total variety of life on Earth (genes, species, ecosystems). **India is biologically diverse** due to highly varied climates, globally hosting 4 out of 36 vital hotspots!
*   **Threats (HIPPO)**: **H**abitat destruction, **I**nvasive species, **P**ollution, **P**opulation overgrowth, and **O**verexploitation. 
*   **Hotspots**: Specific regions that are **extremely rich in unique species** (endemism) but are facing massive imminent destruction. Example: The Western Ghats.
*   **Conservation Strategies**: Creating strict **National Parks and Sanctuaries** (In-situ) or setting up artificial **Seed Banks and Zoos** (Ex-situ) to completely prevent species extinction.

### 7. What Happens If Honey Bees Vanish? *(Assignment 2)*
*   **Crucial Pollinators**: Honey bees perform the critical biological task of **pollinating** roughly one-third of all the human food supply.
*   **The Chain Reaction**: Without bees, **plants fail to reproduce**. Herbivores instantly lose their primary food source and starve, directly collapsing the entire terrestrial **food web**.
*   **Economic Crash**: The total failure of global agriculture would cause massive global **famines** and an uncontrollable economic breakdown, threatening human survival.

### 8. How Bhutan Became Carbon-Negative 
*   **The Status**: Bhutan absorbs exponentially more carbon dioxide than it produces, making it uniquely completely **Carbon-Negative**.
*   **Constitutional Law**: Its brilliant constitution legally mandates that at least **60% of the entire country** must always remain fully covered by untouched **forests**.
*   **Clean Energy Strategy**: Bhutan generates enormous amounts of clean, **renewable hydropower** from rivers and completely restricts heavily polluting industries and commercial logging.

### 9. Water Footprint *(Assignment 3)*
*   **Definition**: The absolute **total volume of freshwater** used directly and indirectly to produce all the goods and services consumed by an individual or a region.
*   **Types**: **Blue Water** (Surface/groundwater consumed), **Green Water** (Rainwater consumed), and **Grey Water** (Fresh water required to dilute generated pollutants).
*   **Virtual Water**: The hidden water literally used in factory production. For example, producing just one **Cotton Jacket** requires thousands of liters of hidden virtual water for growing the cotton and factory dyeing. Production of **Pasta** requires huge amounts of virtual water to aggressively irrigate the wheat.

### 10. Pollution *(Assignment 3)*
*   **Primary Pollutants**: Emitted directly into the air from a specific source (e.g., **CO, SO2** from industrial chimneys or car exhausts). 
*   **Secondary Pollutants**: Formed when primary pollutants react chemically in the air under sunlight (e.g., **Ozone, Photochemical Smog**).
*   **Indoor Pollution**: Toxic air trapped tightly inside homes (from cooking smoke, paints, or poor ventilation). **Sustainable alternative to HVAC**: Utilizing improved **natural cross-ventilation** architectural designs and using **indoor plants** to filter toxins naturally without using electricity.
*   **Acid Rain**: Factory SO2 and NOx mix rapidly with moisture to fall as highly acidic rain, melting limestone monuments and making agricultural soil ungrowable. 
*   **Eutrophication**: Fertilizer continuously runs off into lakes causing massive uncontrollable **algal blooms**, instantly choking off oxygen and suffocating all the fish.

### 11. Effects of Smoking
*   **Personal Health**: Contains incredibly dangerous carcinogens causing severe undeniable **lung cancer**, sudden heart failure, and immediate respiratory breakdown.
*   **Secondhand Smoke**: Emits highly toxic indoor air pollution, forcefully subjecting innocent bystanders and children to inhale deadly unseen chemicals.
*   **Environmental Harm**: Trillions of discarded **cigarette filters** act as non-biodegradable microplastics that severely poison water systems and soil every single year.

### 12. Renewable Energy: Solar & Windmills *(Module 5)*
*   **Solar Energy**: Uses specialized Photovoltaic (PV) cells to instantly turn basic sunlight into direct usable electricity. It is totally **inexhaustible, completely silent**, and 100% emissions-free.
*   **Windmills**: Large aerodynamic turbine blades catch the kinetic energy of moving wind to aggressively spin generators. Highly prominent and efficient in open coastal areas.
*   **The Mission**: Switching to these critical renewables is absolutely essential to stop the burning of fossil fuels and drastically reduce dangerous global **carbon emissions**.

### 13. Electricity Generation from Biomass *(Module 5)*
*   **The Unlimited Source**: Formed directly from discarded organic waste (animal dung, dead agricultural crops, leftover municipal garbage).
*   **Biogas Production Mechanism**: The waste slowly decays naturally using anaerobic bacteria inside large sealed tanks to safely produce combustible **Methane gas**. 
*   **Power Generation**: This methane is then cleanly burned to boil massive tanks of water, create high-pressure steam, and rapidly spin turbines for continuous **electricity**. It powerfully solves both the terrifying waste disposal crisis and energy shortages simultaneously.

### 14. Essential Module 4 Topics: Population & Empowerment 
*   **The IPAT Equation**: Environmental Impact = **Population x Affluence x Technology**. It mathematically proves that a wealthy nation (high affluence/consumerism) causes incredibly more environmental damage than a highly populated but extremely poor nation.
*   **People vs. Consumption Over-population**: Poor developing nations physically suffer from extreme absolute human numbers lacking basic survival food/water, while wealthy developed nations destroy the Earth via severe over-consumption of luxury electronics and vehicles.
*   **Women's Empowerment (The 6S Model)**: Empowering women directly and organically reduces runaway birth rates. The crucial keywords are **Shikshya** (Education), **Swasthya** (Health), **Swavalamban** (Self-Reliance), **Samajik Nyay** (Justice), **Samvedana** (Sensitivity), and **Samata** (Equality).
